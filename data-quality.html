What's missing, and why

Every dataset on this site is a transcription of nineteenth-century paper records. Some of those records were incomplete to start with, some were lost, and some have transcription gaps. This page is the honest map of what's solid and what isn't.

Known gaps in the proposal corpus

The proposal records come from nine annual "Subjects Considered" tables transcribed from BOF annual reports. Several reports have structural gaps in the original transcriptions.

FY1903 through FY1908 have no Board decisions recorded

781 of 1,691 rows · 46% of corpus · status reads "Unknown"

For fiscal years 1903–04 through 1907–08, the source spreadsheets contain the Subject, Proposer, and a researcher's commentary, but the Action column — the Board's actual verdict text — is entirely blank.

Previously these rows defaulted to "Investigating" because the keyword classifier matched nothing. They're now correctly labelled Unknown in the dashboard's status filter (bucketed as "Other"). Approval-rate and rejection-rate charts for those years should be read as having no signal.

BOF Annual Report number typo in FY1907–08

should read AR #20, file says AR #10

Every row in the 1907–08 source spreadsheet has BOF.AR.10.1907-1908 in the report-number column. Annual Report #10 corresponds to FY1899–1900; the correct value for 1907–08 is AR #20. This is a transcription typo in the upstream Excel file, not the pipeline.

AR# column missing year suffix in FY1905–06

column reads "BOF.AR.16 " with no year — broke year extraction

Every other year's source file has the form BOF.AR.N.YYYY-YYYY. The 1905–06 file has only BOF.AR.16 with a trailing space and no year. The pipeline previously left all 133 rows with year = NaN; it now falls back to extracting the year from the filename. Worth normalizing in the source file for consistency.

Known quirks in the budget ledger

Appropriations file extends one year past its filename

master_appropriations_1888_1919.csv ends in 1920

The 1920 row records the Board's dissolution: a zero-dollar entry noting that $230,000 of unspent prior appropriations was returned to the Treasury. It's kept as a marker for the Board's wind-down rather than dropped.
